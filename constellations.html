Constellations

Explore the night sky season by season

Leo

The Lion

One of the few constellations that genuinely resembles its namesake. The backwards question-mark "Sickle" traces the lion's mane while the triangle of stars below forms his haunches.

Brightest Star

What is the brightest star in Leo, and what does it mark on the lion?

tap to flip

Regulus

Blue-white star at the lion's heart. 79 light-years away. Its name means "little king" in Latin.

Mythology

What Greek myth explains Leo's place in the sky?

tap to flip

Leo is the Nemean Lion — the terrifying beast slain by Hercules as his very first labor. Zeus immortalized the fierce creature by placing it among the stars.

How to Find

How do you use the Big Dipper to locate Leo?

tap to flip

Reverse the Big Dipper's pointer stars southward instead of north toward Polaris. Look for the backwards question-mark "Sickle" asterism — that's the lion's mane.

Key Facts

Name a famous deep-sky treasure hidden inside Leo.

tap to flip

The Leo Triplet — three interacting galaxies (M65, M66, NGC 3628) visible through binoculars. Leo is a zodiac constellation best seen in April.

Virgo

The Maiden

The second-largest constellation in the sky, Virgo holds the spike of wheat (Spica) in her left hand. She is linked to harvest and the changing of seasons.

Brightest Star

What is the brightest star in Virgo, and how does it help you navigate the spring sky?

tap to flip

Spica

Blue-white binary star, 250 light-years away. It's the 15th brightest star in the sky and the anchor of the "Spring Triangle" asterism.

Mythology

Which goddess does Virgo represent in Greek mythology?

tap to flip

Classical sources most often identify Virgo with Dike or Astraea, goddess of justice, who was the last immortal to leave Earth as humanity grew wicked. She is also linked to Demeter (goddess of harvest), whose grief over Persephone explains the seasons.

How to Find

What famous mnemonic helps you arc from the Big Dipper to Spica?

tap to flip

"Arc to Arcturus, then speed on to Spica." Follow the curved arc of the Big Dipper's handle outward — two long hops bring you right to bright Spica.

Key Facts

What extraordinary galaxy cluster lurks behind Virgo?

tap to flip

The Virgo Cluster — over 1,300 galaxies packed into a region of sky. It is the heart of our local supercluster of galaxies, about 54–56 million light-years away.

Boötes

The Herdsman

A kite-shaped constellation anchored by Arcturus, one of the brightest stars in the entire sky. Boötes herds the bears Ursa Major and Ursa Minor around the celestial pole.

Brightest Star

What is Arcturus, and why is it special for northern-hemisphere observers?

tap to flip

Arcturus

An orange giant, just 37 light-years away. It is the brightest star in the northern celestial hemisphere and the 4th brightest star in the entire night sky.

Mythology

Who is the herdsman Boötes said to represent?

tap to flip

Most often Arcas — the son of Zeus and Callisto — who nearly killed his mother (transformed into a bear) while hunting. Zeus placed them both in the sky to prevent tragedy.

How to Find

How does the Big Dipper's handle lead you to Boötes?

tap to flip

"Arc to Arcturus" — follow the sweeping curve of the Big Dipper's handle outward. The bright orange star you reach first is Arcturus, heart of Boötes.

Key Facts

What is unusual about Arcturus's journey through the galaxy?

tap to flip

Arcturus is a high-velocity star moving in a different direction than most local stars, suggesting it originated in a dwarf galaxy absorbed by the Milky Way long ago.

Corvus

The Crow

A small but distinctive quadrilateral of four stars low in the spring sky. Despite its modest size, Corvus's compact trapezoid shape makes it surprisingly easy to identify.

Brightest Star

What is the brightest star in Corvus?

tap to flip

Gienah

A blue-white giant about 154 light-years away. Its name derives from the Arabic for "wing of the crow."

Mythology

Why did Apollo punish the crow in the Corvus myth?

tap to flip

Apollo sent his sacred crow to fetch water. The crow stopped to wait for figs to ripen, then lied and blamed a water snake for the delay. Apollo banished the bird to the sky, forever thirsty beside the cup (Crater).

How to Find

How do you locate tiny Corvus in the spring sky?

tap to flip

Find bright Spica in Virgo, then look west (right). The small, lopsided quadrilateral of four stars sitting just south of the sky highway is Corvus.

Key Facts

What famous pair of colliding galaxies lies near Corvus?

tap to flip

The Antennae Galaxies (NGC 4038/4039) — two spiral galaxies in the midst of a violent collision, stretching tidal tails of stars across space. A striking target through telescopes.

Scorpius

The Scorpion

One of the few constellations that truly looks like its namesake — a sweeping curve of bright stars ending in a curved stinger tail. Best seen hugging the southern horizon on summer nights.

Brightest Star

What gives Scorpius's heart its distinctive red color?

tap to flip

Antares

A red supergiant about 550 light-years away, so large it would swallow Mars's orbit. Its name means "rival of Mars" because of its ruddy color.

Mythology

Why are Orion and Scorpius placed on opposite sides of the sky?

tap to flip

In Greek myth, the goddess Gaia sent the scorpion to slay the boastful hunter Orion. The gods honored both by placing them in the sky — but forever on opposite horizons so they never meet again.

How to Find

Where and when should you look for Scorpius?

tap to flip

Look low along the southern horizon on summer evenings. Find Antares — the bright, distinctly reddish star — then trace the arc of stars curving into the scorpion's tail.

Key Facts

What makes the region around Scorpius extraordinary through binoculars?

tap to flip

Scorpius lies along the Milky Way, packed with open star clusters. M6 (Butterfly Cluster) and M7 (Ptolemy Cluster) are among the finest in the sky — easily visible without a telescope.

Cygnus

The Swan

A large, cross-shaped constellation known as the Northern Cross. Cygnus flies south along the bright Milky Way, with the brilliant star Deneb marking the tail.

Brightest Star

Why is Deneb extraordinary despite appearing less bright than its Summer Triangle partners?

tap to flip

Deneb

A white supergiant roughly 1,500 light-years away — one of the most luminous stars known. If it were as close as Vega (25 ly), it would cast shadows at night.

Mythology

What two myths explain Cygnus in the sky?

tap to flip

Most often, Zeus transformed himself into a swan to court Leda. Alternatively, Cygnus is Orpheus — the legendary musician who was transformed into a swan after his death and placed beside his lyre (Lyra).

How to Find

How does the Summer Triangle help you find Cygnus?

tap to flip

Deneb is one of the three stars of the Summer Triangle (with Vega and Altair). It marks the top of the Northern Cross asterism — a large cross of stars lying along the Milky Way.

Key Facts

What famous black hole and nebula are hidden inside Cygnus?

tap to flip

Cygnus X-1 is one of the most studied black holes, first identified as a strong black hole candidate in 1972 and definitively confirmed by 2011. The North America Nebula (NGC 7000) glows in the shape of the continent just east of Deneb.

Lyra

The Lyre

A small but unmistakable constellation anchored by dazzling Vega. The tiny parallelogram of stars below Vega represents the frame of the ancient instrument of Orpheus.

Brightest Star

What makes Vega one of the most significant stars in astronomy?

tap to flip

Vega

A blue-white star only 25 light-years away and the 5th brightest in the night sky. Vega was the first star ever photographed (1850) and will be our north pole star in about 12,000 years.

Mythology

Whose instrument does Lyra represent, and what became of it?

tap to flip

The lyre of Orpheus, the greatest musician who ever lived. When Orpheus was killed by the Maenads, the gods retrieved his lyre and placed it in the heavens as an eternal tribute to his art.

How to Find

How do you locate Lyra on a summer night?

tap to flip

Vega is the brightest star high overhead on summer evenings and the topmost point of the Summer Triangle. Below Vega, spot the small parallelogram of fainter stars — that is the lyre itself.

Key Facts

What famous nebula can you find inside Lyra with a telescope?

tap to flip

The Ring Nebula (M57) — a perfect smoke-ring of glowing gas, the shell ejected by a dying star. It sits between the bottom two stars of the lyre's parallelogram and is a classic telescope target.

Aquila

The Eagle

The eagle of Zeus, flying along the Milky Way. Altair, its blazing eye, is the nearest of the Summer Triangle stars and one of the fastest-rotating bright stars known.

Brightest Star

What is remarkable about Altair's rotation speed?

tap to flip

Altair

Just 17 light-years away, Altair spins so fast (once every ~7.7 hours vs. the Sun's 25 days) that it bulges at the equator — it's over 20% wider than tall. The first main-sequence star (other than the Sun) to have its surface directly resolved, in 2006.

Mythology

What two roles does the eagle play in Greek mythology?

tap to flip

Aquila carries Zeus's thunderbolts, ready to deliver divine punishment. In another tale, it is the eagle that daily tore out Prometheus's liver — punishment for stealing fire and giving it to humanity.

How to Find

Which Summer Triangle star belongs to Aquila, and what helps confirm it?

tap to flip

Altair is the southernmost point of the Summer Triangle. Confirm it by the two flanking stars — Tarazed (brighter, orange) and Alshain (fainter) — one on each side, forming a distinctive trio.

Key Facts

What does Altair's proximity mean for observers?

tap to flip

At 17 light-years, Altair is one of the closest bright stars visible to the naked eye. The light entering your eye tonight left Altair in 2009. It lies in a rich stretch of the Milky Way.

Pegasus

The Winged Horse

The Great Square of Pegasus dominates the fall sky — four stars forming a nearly perfect square visible even in light-polluted skies. Only the front half of the horse is depicted in the stars.

Brightest Star

What is the brightest star in Pegasus and where is it?

tap to flip

Enif

An orange supergiant about 690 light-years away, marking the horse's nose. Its name is Arabic for "the nose." Despite being Epsilon (ε) Pegasi, it outshines all other stars in the constellation.

Mythology

How was Pegasus born, and what hero rode him?

tap to flip

Pegasus sprang from the blood of Medusa when Perseus beheaded her. The hero Bellerophon later tamed Pegasus with a golden bridle and rode him to slay the fire-breathing Chimera.

How to Find

How do you use the Great Square to navigate the fall sky?

tap to flip

The Great Square of Pegasus — four stars in a large, nearly perfect square — is the anchor of the fall sky. Find it high overhead in October. It's larger than you expect; about the size of your fist at arm's length.

Key Facts

What lies nearby that a naked eye can find on a dark night?

tap to flip

The globular cluster M15 lies just northwest of Enif — one of the most densely packed star clusters known, with over 100,000 stars. On a clear dark night, it's visible to the naked eye as a fuzzy point.

Andromeda

The Chained Princess

A constellation of two arcing chains of stars stretching from the Great Square of Pegasus. It contains the most distant object visible to the naked eye — the Andromeda Galaxy.

Brightest Star

Which star anchors both Andromeda and the Great Square of Pegasus?

tap to flip

Alpheratz

A blue subgiant about 97 light-years away. It sits at the top-left corner of the Great Square while also marking Andromeda's head — shared by both constellations officially until 1930.

Mythology

Why was Andromeda chained to the rocks?

tap to flip

Her mother, Queen Cassiopeia, boasted that Andromeda was more beautiful than the sea nymphs. Poseidon was furious and sent the sea monster Cetus to ravage the coast. Only sacrificing Andromeda could appease him — until Perseus arrived.

How to Find

How do you star-hop from the Great Square to the Andromeda Galaxy?

tap to flip

From Alpheratz (top-left of Great Square), follow two stars northeast, then turn slightly north. The Andromeda Galaxy (M31) appears as an elongated fuzzy patch — larger than the full Moon in actual sky size.

Key Facts

What is so astonishing about the light from the Andromeda Galaxy?

tap to flip

M31 is 2.5 million light-years away — the light you see tonight left before modern humans existed on Earth. It is the farthest thing visible to the naked eye and is approaching us at 110 km/s. In ~4.5 billion years it will collide with the Milky Way.

Perseus

The Hero

The legendary hero flies between Cassiopeia and Auriga, holding aloft the severed head of Medusa (marked by the "Demon Star" Algol). Perseus is rich with star clusters and mythology.

Brightest Star

What is the brightest star in Perseus, and what does it mark?

tap to flip

Mirfak

A yellow-white supergiant about 510 light-years away, marking Perseus's torso. It is surrounded by a loose cluster of young stars (the Alpha Persei Cluster) visible through binoculars.

Mythology

What three heroic deeds is Perseus famous for?

tap to flip

Perseus slew the Gorgon Medusa (using a mirrored shield to avoid her petrifying gaze), rescued Andromeda from the sea monster Cetus, and used Medusa's severed head to turn Cetus to stone.

How to Find

What makes Algol a uniquely interesting star to observe over time?

tap to flip

Algol (β Per) is the "Demon Star" — an eclipsing binary that visibly dims by over a full magnitude every 2.87 days as the fainter companion passes in front. Watch it for a few nights and see it change.

Key Facts

What spectacular pair of star clusters lies at Perseus's sword hand?

tap to flip

The Double Cluster (NGC 869 and NGC 884) — two open clusters side by side, each containing hundreds of young blue-white stars. They are one of the most beautiful binocular objects in the entire sky.

Cassiopeia

The Queen

The unmistakable W or M shape near the north pole makes Cassiopeia one of the easiest constellations to find. It is circumpolar — never setting from most of the Northern Hemisphere.

Brightest Star

What is the brightest star in Cassiopeia?

tap to flip

Schedar

An orange giant about 230 light-years away marking the queen's breast. Its name comes from Arabic for "the breast." The constellation's brightness shifts as Gamma Cassiopeiae (an eruptive variable) fluctuates.

Mythology

What was Queen Cassiopeia's fatal flaw?

tap to flip

Vanity. She boasted that she and her daughter Andromeda were more beautiful than the Nereids (sea nymphs). Poseidon, outraged on behalf of the nymphs, punished Ethiopia — and as further humiliation, placed Cassiopeia in the sky forever spinning around the pole.

How to Find

How does Cassiopeia serve as a year-round navigation aid?

tap to flip

Cassiopeia's W shape sits on the opposite side of Polaris from the Big Dipper. When the Dipper is low or hidden, use Cassiopeia to find the North Star — draw a line from the middle of the W and it points toward Polaris.

Key Facts

What exploded in Cassiopeia in 1572 and astonished astronomers?

tap to flip

Tycho's Star — a supernova so bright it was visible in daylight for weeks. Tycho Brahe's careful observations of this "new star" helped disprove the ancient belief that the heavens were unchanging and perfect.

Orion

The Hunter

The king of the winter sky and perhaps the most recognized constellation on Earth. Three equally-spaced belt stars flanked by red Betelgeuse and blue Rigel create an unmistakable hourglass pattern.

Brightest Star

Which star is actually the brightest in Orion, despite not being "Alpha"?

tap to flip

Rigel

A blue supergiant about 860 light-years away and 120,000 times more luminous than the Sun. It is designated Beta (β) Orionis but outshines the more famous Betelgeuse.

Mythology

Why do Orion and Scorpius never share the sky at the same time?

tap to flip

Orion was a great hunter who boasted no creature could defeat him. Gaia, the earth goddess, sent a scorpion that killed him. Zeus placed both in the sky but on opposite sides, so they perpetually chase each other — when one rises, the other sets.

How to Find

How do Orion's belt stars serve as a cosmic compass?

tap to flip

The three belt stars (Alnitak, Alnilam, Mintaka) point in two directions: extend them left/east to find Sirius (the brightest star in the sky); extend them right/west to find Aldebaran and the V-shaped Hyades in Taurus.

Key Facts

What stellar nursery hangs below Orion's belt as his "sword"?

tap to flip

The Orion Nebula (M42) — a glowing cloud of gas and dust 1,344 light-years away where stars are being born right now. To the naked eye it is a fuzzy star in the sword; through binoculars, it is breathtaking.

Taurus

The Bull

A V-shaped cluster of stars forms the bull's face, with fiery Aldebaran glaring from its eye. Two iconic star clusters — the Hyades and Pleiades — make Taurus one of the richest constellations to explore.

Brightest Star

What color is Aldebaran and what does it tell us about the star?

tap to flip

Aldebaran

A red giant about 65 light-years away and 44 times the Sun's diameter. Its orange-red color signals it has exhausted its core hydrogen and expanded — a preview of our Sun's fate in 5 billion years.

Mythology

Which god disguised himself as a bull, and why?

tap to flip

Zeus transformed into a magnificent white bull to approach the Phoenician princess Europa on the beach. She climbed onto his back, and he swam across the sea to Crete — giving Europe its name. The bull was honored in the stars.

How to Find

How do Orion's belt stars lead you directly to Taurus?

tap to flip

Follow Orion's three belt stars rightward (westward). The V-shaped Hyades star cluster — the bull's face — comes into view, with orange Aldebaran as its eye. Continue further and you'll reach the Pleiades (Seven Sisters) cluster.

Key Facts

What famous supernova remnant is hiding inside Taurus?

tap to flip

The Crab Nebula (M1) — the gaseous remnant of a supernova witnessed and recorded by Chinese and Japanese astronomers in 1054 AD. At its center spins a pulsar, a neutron star rotating 30 times per second.

Gemini

The Twins

Two parallel rows of stars stretch from a shared foot near Orion up to the brilliant twin heads of Castor and Pollux. Gemini sits in a rich stretch of the Milky Way.

Brightest Star

Which twin star is actually brighter, and why is it surprising?

tap to flip

Pollux

Pollux (β Gem) is slightly brighter than Castor (α Gem), which is unusual — typically the alpha star is the brightest. Pollux is an orange giant 34 light-years away and has a confirmed exoplanet orbiting it.

Mythology

What made the twin brothers Castor and Pollux inseparable even in death?

tap to flip

Castor was mortal (son of the king) while Pollux was immortal (son of Zeus). When Castor died in battle, Pollux was so grief-stricken that Zeus allowed them to share immortality — each spending alternate days on Olympus and in the underworld.

How to Find

How do you locate Gemini relative to Orion?

tap to flip

Gemini lies northeast of Orion. Find Betelgeuse (Orion's red shoulder) and continue that line northeast — you'll arrive at Castor and Pollux, the bright twin heads. They are noticeably different in color: Castor is white, Pollux is orange.

Key Facts

What secret is hiding inside the star named Castor?

tap to flip

Castor appears as one star to the naked eye, but through a telescope it splits into two. Each of those two stars also has a companion, and there is a third pair of red dwarfs in the system. Castor is actually six stars orbiting each other.

Canis Major

The Greater Dog

Orion's faithful hunting dog leaps along the southern winter sky, with Sirius — the single brightest star in the entire night sky — blazing at its collar like a jewel.

Brightest Star

By how much does Sirius outshine the next brightest star?

tap to flip

Sirius

At magnitude -1.46, Sirius is nearly twice as bright as Canopus (the second brightest). It is a white star only 8.6 light-years away — one of the Sun's closest neighbors. It has a white dwarf companion, Sirius B.

Mythology

How did Sirius influence ancient Egyptian civilization?

tap to flip

The ancient Egyptians called Sirius "Sopdet." Its heliacal rising — first appearance above the horizon just before sunrise — announced the annual flooding of the Nile, which fertilized crops. Sirius effectively started the Egyptian calendar year.

How to Find

How does Orion's belt act as a pointer to Sirius?

tap to flip

Follow Orion's three belt stars downward and to the left (eastward and south). The first brilliant star you reach — far brighter than anything nearby — is Sirius. You truly cannot miss it; it often flickers with rainbow colors near the horizon.

Key Facts

What are the "Dog Days of Summer" and why is Canis Major responsible?

tap to flip

The Romans noticed that the hottest stretch of summer (July–August) coincided with Sirius rising alongside the Sun. They believed Sirius added its heat to the Sun's — creating the "dies caniculares," the dog days. Now we know it's a coincidence of Earth's orbit.
